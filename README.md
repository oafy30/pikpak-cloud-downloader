## What Does "Save Link to Cloud" Actually Mean?

Most people think of cloud storage as a place you upload things to. Google Drive, Dropbox, iCloud — you drag files in, they appear on your phone and laptop. That's fine for documents and photos.

But "save link to cloud" is a different idea entirely. Instead of uploading a file, you hand the cloud service a *link* — a torrent, a magnet link, a social media URL — and the cloud service's own servers go fetch the file. The download happens remotely, on enterprise-grade hardware, at speeds that have nothing to do with your home internet connection.

When the file lands in your cloud, you can:

- Stream it directly (video in up to 4K quality)
- Download it to any device, any time
- Share it with others via a link
- Access it from your phone, tablet, desktop, or TV

This is the model PikPak built itself around, and it's genuinely different from what Google Drive or Dropbox does.
